LPENHS History

A journey of growth, dedication, and quality education.

1986: Humble Beginnings

Las Pinas East National High School started brightening lives when the principal of CAA Elementary School, Mr. Zoro Zapanta, decided to widen the scope of his school by welcoming high school students. He started with a humble beginning of two first-year sections and a second-year section – all of which held classes after the dismissal of the elementary students.

1989: A Dedicated Campus

Enrollment continued increasing. Mrs. Purificacion Aranda and Mrs. Ma. Luisa Santos referred to then municipal mayor, Hon. Rosalino Riguera, for an adequate school site. Resolution No. 116.89 was adopted, granting a 4,000 square meter open space in Verdant Acres Subdivision. A 10-classroom, 2-storey building immediately followed.

1990 - 1991: Opening Doors

After laborious efforts, including 100,000.00 for comfort rooms and 56,000.00 for electrical systems sanctioned by the mayor, LPENHS finally opened its doors for all qualified high school students in S.Y. 1990-1991.

During the direction of Dr. Isabelita Z. Montesa, the DPWH Building came to existence—a 2-storey building with 6 rooms (1 industrial and 5 academic) amounting to 5,131,960.00 from National funds.

1991 - 1993: Independence

Ms. Maria Lucia R. Santos succeeded in acquiring the school’s independence funded by the Local gov’t. S.Y. 1992-1993 saw 2,615 enrollees and 334 graduates. During 1992, the Pimentel Bldg was added: a 2-storey building housing canteens and faculties, costing 3,462,840.00.

1993 - 1999: Reaching National Status

Supervised by Ms. Andrelina J. Villanueva, the school saw rapid growth, reaching 4,964 enrollees by S.Y. 1997-1998. Infrastructures like SEDP 1, SEDP 2 (1993), and the impressive 3-storey Aguilar bldg (1996) with 15 rooms were constructed.

1997 Milestone: After bravely tackling all hurdles, the Department of Education recognized the school’s accomplishments and awarded it National Status.

2002 - 2008: Continuous Expansion

Under Mrs. Eugenia V. Guerra, enrollment peaked at over 6,165 students. Further expansion included the "New Bldg" in 2006 and the "Principal Led" building in 2007 to accommodate the massive student body and enhance educational facilities.

Present Day

Today, Las Pinas East National High School has tackled all endeavors and brought all the lessons with its every trod. Presently managed by Ms. Phondora S. Capistrano, the school continually grows. Up to now, it remains faithful to its calling – providing quality education and the best student training to produce globally competent students that our society will be proud of.

Source: The Blue Palm Archives
